Reimagining the Internet

The Internet We Lost

The early vision of the internet was a space of freedom and creativity, where netizens could collaborate, share ideas, and innovate without restrictions. Works like Hackers (1995) encapsulate this spirit, portraying a decentralized digital frontier built by and for the people.

The Internet We Have

Today, the internet is dominated by corporations and billionaires like Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, Jeff Bezos, and Bill Gates. Platforms prioritize profit, turning users into products through surveillance and data monetization. This shift has fostered manipulation, polarization, and exploitation, straying far from the internet's original promise.

The Internet We Need

To reclaim the internet, we must embrace decentralized technologies and community-driven platforms. An internet in the hands of netizens would prioritize transparency, creativity, privacy, and ethical design. Technologies like blockchain and federated systems offer hope for a freer, fairer web.

Actions for Change

Reimagining the internet requires a collective effort. Here are key steps:

  • Promote open-source and decentralized platforms.
  • Advocate for policies ensuring data sovereignty and privacy.
  • Support equitable access to digital resources.
